Introduction to R - Background and Downloading

What is R?

According to the R website, "R is a system for statistical computation and graphics." Essentially, this means R is something similar to SAS, SPSS or STATA.

But R is also much more than this.

  • R is data analysis software.
  • R is a programming language.
  • R is an environment for statistical analysis.
  • R is an open-source software project.
  • R is a community. *

Why do people use R?

Personaly, I like R better than SAS or SPSS. I couldn't quite put my finger on the reasons why, so I did my research and here are the most common answers I found.

    Reasons people love R

  • It's free.

  • Regular updates.

  • It's an interactive language.

  • Excellent graphics.

  • Handles missing values nicely.

  • The extensive library of pre-existing packages.

  • The community.

    Reasons people strongly dislike R

  • It's difficult to learn.
  • It's not an intutitive language (e.g. the assignment operator and case sensitivity).
  • It's uses your computer's RAM for data handling.
  • Apparently, it doesn't interact pleasantly with Excel.




Installing R

Firstly, you need to download a version of R. If you go to the R website, and follow the link to the relevant country and operating system, you can easily download R. (Probably best to pick the newest one, unless you specically need an older version)

Next, I would advise downloading R Studio (more on the whys later). Again, just visit the R Studio website and choose your operating system to download a version.
